Overview
The fields of engineering and computing offer many career paths, and it can be challenging for a student to choose which one is right for them. MSOE has created Explorer Pathways to help first year students decide which program of study and career path is the best fit for their skills and interests. Explorer Pathways students meet industry practitioners and take coursework from multiple degree programs to become empowered to make an informed decision about their future.
Engineering Explorers Pathway
Jump to
Computing Explorers Pathway
The Engineering Explorers Pathway is a one-year program. In the first semester of classes, students receive an introduction to engineering and related areas of study, conduct career exploration activities, and gain college success skills. In their second semester of classes, students take authentic in-major courses in multiple majors of interest. Students are introduced to all engineering programs, user experience, computer science, construction management and business and decide on the program that is best for them at the end of their first year of study.
Program Benefits
Try out several engineering disciplines so you can find the one that fits you best
Courses count towards degree in most programs and in engineering minors
Enjoy community building activities with peers in the Explorers Pathway
An advisor helps students create a custom four-year graduation plan and supports students throughout their academic career

Exploring Engineering Course
Learn about the different disciplines of engineering and related fields through lectures, hands-on activities, career research, and panel discussions with upper-class students, alumni, industry professionals and faculty

Meet regularly with an academic advisor and RCAS Raider Success Ally who can assess student their needs and connects them with campus resources

Computing Explorers Pathway
The Computing Explorers Pathway is a one-year program. In the first semester of classes, students will receive a broad introduction to computing through courses in computer history, digital logic, and user experience. In their second semester of classes, students take a software course and an elective course to further explore their interests in computing.   Students are introduced to MSOE’s four computing programs, computer science, computer engineering, software engineering and user experience, and decide on the program that is best for them at the end of their first year of study.
Program Benefits
Try out several computing majors so you can find the one that fits you best
All courses count towards degree requirements in most programs
Enjoy community building activities with peers in the Explorers Pathway
An advisor helps students create a custom four-year graduation plan and supports students throughout their academic career
3
Computing History Course
Learn about the history of computing, beginning with the abacus and ancient stories of machines, through mechanical and electronic computers to the fields of modern computing
Learn about the social forces and techno-cultural innovations that shaped the field
Study developments in hardware, software, and design through lectures, case studies, guest speakers, media, and hands-on experimentation
Explore areas of personal interest and learn about the modern fields of computer engineering, computer science, software engineering, and user experience

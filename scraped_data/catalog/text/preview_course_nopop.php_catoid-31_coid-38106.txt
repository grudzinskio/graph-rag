BME 1010 - Introduction to Biomedical Engineering
1
lecture hours
2
lab hours
2
credits
Course Description
This course introduces students to the biomedical engineering (BME) profession. Students are introduced to different subdisciplines and career options in BME. The BME curriculum is discussed, along with strategies for choosing among different curricular and co-curricular options to best be prepared for the different career options. Students are also introduced to the medical device design process and engage in a group project to design and fabricate a medical device component based on realistic specifications. This course meets the following Raider Core CLO requirement:
Integrate Learning
.   (prereq: none)
Course Learning Outcomes
Upon successful completion of this course, the student will be able to:
Name the characteristics of a medical device
Identify the requirements and different options for completing the biomedical engineering curriculum
Identify medical devices associated with the major sub-areas of biomedical engineering
Identify career options for biomedical engineers and how choices made as a biomedical engineering student may influence one’s options upon graduation
Identify different types of engineering considerations that go into the design of common biomedical devices
Explain the importance of regulation of the medical device industry
Explain the importance of human factors and safety considerations in medical device design
Work in a team to design and fabricate a medical device component, based on given specifications
Prerequisites by Topic
None
Course Topics
Defining a medical device
Sub-disciplines and career options of biomedical engineering
The biomedical engineering curriculum
Introduction to artificial intelligence and data science in the healthcare industry
Introduction to the medical device design process and identification of requirements
Introduction to medical device regulation
Human factors and safety considerations in medical device design
Introduction to computer-aided design
Introduction to voltage, current and resistance in DC electrical circuits
Principles of biopotential measurement
Laboratory Topics
Artificial intelligence for image classification
Computer-aided design
3D printing and other fabrication methods
Voltage and resistance measurement
Voltage divider applications
Measurement of biopotentials
Medical device component design and fabrication
Coordinator
Dr. Jeff LaMack

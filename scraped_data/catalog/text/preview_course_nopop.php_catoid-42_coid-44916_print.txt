3
lecture hours
0
lab hours
3
credits
Course Description
This course explores American military history from the colonial period to the present through the lens of military affairs and primarily through the land component of the military, the Army. This course will use the Army and the military itself as a lens through which to explore the impact of governmental structures and policies, international affairs, societal change, technological and industrial innovation, and geography on American development.
Prereq:
ROTC department chair consent
Note:
MU: MISL 1800. For Army ROTC students only

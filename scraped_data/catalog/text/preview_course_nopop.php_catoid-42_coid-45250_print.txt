BUS 3445 - Leadership Communication
3
lecture hours
0
lab hours
3
credits
Course Description
Do you have what it takes to be a leader? You may not think so, but everyone can be a leader, but not every leader can be successful in every setting. This class addresses leadership styles and skills across various situations and provides students the opportunity to explore and develop leadership and leadership communication. Course activities will move the student from theory to the practical processes of leadership, effective and ethical leadership communication tools, and increase your effectiveness as a leader using your personality and strengths. Basic concepts essential to leadership skills development, motivational theories, and interpersonal relationship management are key building blocks in this course.
Prereq:
Sophomore standing
Note:
None
This course meets the following Raider Core CLO Requirement:
None
Course Learning Outcomes
Upon successful completion of this course, the student will be able to:
Evaluate individual leadership strengths and weaknesses in various situations
Demonstrate leadership communication strategies across various situations and platforms using written and verbal modalities
Apply cultural sensitivity when communicating in diverse groups and organizations
Debate use of motivational strategies, personal power sources, and interpersonal politics to lead effectively
Exemplify interpersonal skills related to leadership communication: impromptu communication, professional opinion sharing, navigating crucial conversations, and ethics in communication
Prerequisites by Topic
None
Course Topics
Leadership styles
Personality assessments, strategies, and reflections
Crucial conversations strategies and tactics
Trust
Followership
Power and politics
Multicultural differences in leadership
Leader roles and skills
Communication modality assessment and application
Diversity, cultural, and global communication reviews
Persuasive and professional communication
Communication etiquette
Formal business writing and etiquette
Leadership and communication ethics
Coordinator
Dr. Trina Moskalik

EGR 1001 - Exploring Engineering
2
lecture hours
2
lab hours
3
credits
Course Description
Students will learn about the different disciplines of engineering and related fields through lectures, discussions, research, hands-on activities, and interacting with MSOE community members working in the disciplines. Students will engage in student-centered active-learning exercises focused on career exploration and student success. Students will decide which fields might be a great match for them and work with an advisor to create a course plan for further exploration in their majors of interest. Students will choose in-major courses to enroll in for the following term.
Prereq:
None
Note:
Only students in the Engineering Explorers Pathway program may enroll
This course meets the following Raider Core CLO Requirement:
None
Course Learning Outcomes
Upon successful completion of this course, the student will be able to:
Explore strengths, values, skills, and interests that influence major and career choice
Recognize differences between fields of study at MSOE
Critically evaluate degree options by utilizing self-reflection, career research, and decision-making strategies
Research and describe careers and day to day work associated with fields of study of interest to the student
Investigate career options through interaction with the MSOE community and its partners
Demonstrate understanding of one’s own career aspirations and fit with MSOE majors
Determine specific majors of interest at MSOE to pursue in the following term
Prerequisites by Topic
None

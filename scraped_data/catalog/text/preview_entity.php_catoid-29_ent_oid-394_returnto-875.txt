The Civil and Architectural Engineering and Construction Management (CAECM) Department offers a Bachelor of Science in Architectural Engineering (AE), a Bachelor of Science in Construction Management (CM), a Bachelor of Science in Civil Engineering (CV), a Master of Science in Architectural Engineering and a Master of Science in Civil Engineering. The CAECM Department also has an option for a five-year, freshman-to-master’s degree program for both civil engineering and for architectural engineering, for which students must apply at the end of their third year.  The department also offers a five-year, double-major option, which allows students to earn both the B.S. in Architechtural Engineering and B.S. in Construction Management degrees.
The AE bachelor’s degree program has concentrations in building structural, mechanical and electrical systems design. The CM bachelor’s degree program emphasizes building estimating, scheduling and project management for commercial and industrial building projects. The CV bachelor’s degree program offers specializations in structural engineering, environmental and water resources engineering, transportation engineering, and construction management.
All degree programs within the CAECM department are supported by extensive design studios and laboratory facilities.

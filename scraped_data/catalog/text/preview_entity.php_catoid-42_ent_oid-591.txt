The Dwight & Dian Diercks School of Advanced Computing is housed in the Dwight and Dian Diercks Computational Science Hall. Undergraduate degree-granting responsibility includes the areas of computer science and software engineering as well as minors in data science and cybersecurity. The department offers a graduate degree program in machine learning and certificate programs in applied machine learning and machine learning engineering. The department maintains eight engineering and computer science laboratories, a high-performance computing cluster, and a corporate partnership center.

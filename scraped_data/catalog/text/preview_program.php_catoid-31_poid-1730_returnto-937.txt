Overview
A computer engineering minor broadens expertise in the architectural design of computer hardware as well as the hardware and software techniques used in embedded systems design. For non-majors, adding the computer engineering minor can increase skillset and make graduates more attractive to potential employers as well as enhance preparedness for graduate school. To qualify for a Minor in Computer Engineering (CPE), a student must take at least 18 credits of eligible courses.

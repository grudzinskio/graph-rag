Dwight and Dian Diercks School of Advanced Computing
Learn to write cutting-edge software, solve problems with artificial intelligence and machine learning, and learn on the leading edge of a technical revolution.
Located in Dwight and Dian Diercks Computational Science Hall, which is also home to Rosie the supercomputer, students in the Diercks School of Advanced Computing will develop skills to solve problems using algorithms, applied mathematics and AI in a hands-on environment working with experienced faculty. The systems you’ll develop benefit virtually every part of our lives and achieve goals that were previously impossible.

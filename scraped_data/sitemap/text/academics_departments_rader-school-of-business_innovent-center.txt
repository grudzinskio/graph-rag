MSOE Innovent Center
The Uihlein/Spitzer Center for Innovation and Entrepreneurship (also known as the “Innovent Center”) promotes entrepreneurship inspiration, education and collaboration at MSOE.
What is the Innovent Center?
The Innovent Center is dedicated to interdisciplinary exploration and collaboration. It’s a space for MSOE students, alumni, faculty and staff to work, experiment, brainstorm, collaborate, learn, study and work together. Innovent staff provides entrepreneurial and intrapreneurial inspiration, education and mentoring through:
business concept refinement
ideation assistance
intellectual property development
financial resource identification
market identification and feasibility
business plan development
Apply for seed funds to support your projects!
“The Innovent Center welcomes those who are in the early stages of ideation. It is a place to grab a cup of coffee and a snack, meet likeminded individuals as well as those from other disciplines, make connections and find resources.” - Dr. Ruth Barratt, chair, Rader School of Business
What's at the Innovent Center?
Users have the opportunity to connect with one another, tinker, hear from guest speakers, receive guidance and more. There are 3D printers, sewing machines, photography lightboxes, vinyl cutters, an arcade game and more available for the MSOE community to use as they bring their ideas to life.
The Innovent Center is located in
Rosenberg Hall, R-101
and is open Monday through Friday, 9 a.m. to 5 p.m. For more information, email

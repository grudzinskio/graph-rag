The Office of the Dean of Students
Fostering student development.
The Dean of Students Office works to advocate, support and develop students as they navigate their time here at MSOE. Additionally, we serve as a resource for faculty and staff as well as parents and families as they seek to educate and support our students. We support student well-being and success by providing care, promoting accountability and encouraging individual growth and development.
We pride ourselves on being an office where students, faculty and families will find an answer to their question, a solution to their problem or be connected to the office or individual who can best provide assistance.
Here are some common ways the Dean of Students Office can support students’ journeys as a MSOE Raider.
Single point of contact within the university for students and families in times of emergency or crisis
Helping students navigate through difficult situations and appropriate referrals to on and off-campus resources that can best support students
Consultation and referrals for students contemplating withdrawing or taking a leave of absence from the university
Maintaining community standards and facilitating the university’s conduct process
Creating a sense of belonging where all students feel safe and cared about
Help emerging adults develop academically, socially and emotionally as they navigate the transition to adulthood
Programs and Services Provided:
Student Advocacy and Support
Help with personal, academic or financial crises
Assist students navigating complex campus systems or policies
Mediate conflicts between students and faculty or staff
Student Conduct and Discipline
Oversee the student code of conduct
Address violations of academic integrity or behavioral standards
Educate students about their rights and responsibilities
Crisis Response and Case Management
Provide support for students in distress (mental health, housing insecurity, medical emergencies)
Coordinate referrals to counseling, health services or community resources
Title IX and Equity Support
Respond to complaints related to sexual harassment, discrimination or assault
Help students access interim measures, like class changes or no-contact orders
Absence Notifications and Emergency Leave
Notify professors of prolonged absences due to illness, family emergencies or other legitimate reasons
Help students apply for medical or emergency leave from school
Student Engagement and Leadership
Support student organizations and clubs
Coordinate leadership development programs, student government and campus events
Support for Special Populations
First-generation college students
LGBTQ+ students
Veterans or military-connected students
Students with disabilities
Behavioral Intervention and Threat Assessment
Participate in a campus-wide CARE team or behavioral intervention team to address safety concerns and connect students to help

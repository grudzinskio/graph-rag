STEM Organizations
Flex your STEM muscle in one of our many Engineering and Robotic organizations.
MSOE provides students a wide variety range of organizations to showcase classroom skills in real world applications and competitions. Many of MSOE’s STEM Organizations win national and international awards in their fields.
Visit the
WE Energies STEM Center at MSOE webpages
to learn about the wonderful facility we have on campus to support STEM.
Competitive Computing Club
This organization focuses on the development of technical skills in Cybersecurity and Programming through friendly competition, professional development, and workshops.
Concrete Canoe
Concrete Canoe is a student run organization that spends the whole year designing and constructing a canoe made out of light weight concrete. Concrete Canoe provides students a hands-on experience with concrete mix design and project management challenges.
Finance and Investment Club
The Finance & Investment Society is an organization committed to promoting financial literacy, investment education, and professional development. Through interactive workshops, guest speaker events, and real-world applications, the society provides members with the skills and insights needed to succeed in finance-related careers. Our mission is to create a collaborative environment where students can explore financial markets, gain practical experience, and build meaningful industry connections.
High Powered Rocketry Club (HPRC)
The MSOE High Powered Rocketry Club (HPRC) is a group of students fueled by curiosity and a love for rockets. Every major is welcoming, we offer learning and collaborative opportunities in designing, programming, testing, and launching rockets. HPRC also competes in various rocketry competitions run by the Tripoli Rocket Association across the nation.
Midnight Robotics
Midnight Robotics seeks to create engineering opportunities for students by creating an organization that can facilitate the growth, understanding, and real world application of engineering principles/concepts through the design, fabrication, and testing of combat robots.
MSOE Artificial Intelligence Club (MAIC)
MSOE AI Club (MAIC) is built upon a foundation of teaching as many people as possible about the innovative space of artificial intelligence, regardless of their previous experience. We do this through a combination of Speaker Events, Innovation Labs, and Research Groups.
MSOE Game Development
The MSOE Game Development Club strives to create an environment where students can use the skills they've learned in a creatively engaging way, while providing them an escape from the hectic life of MSOE.
Raider 3D
Raider 3D is an organization that gives members of the MSOE community the opportunity to learn, share and collaborate ideas in the field of 3D Printing by hosting events and workshops, participating in competitions and other educational activities.
Raider Robotics VEX U
Raider Robotics is a student-led VEX U Competitive Robotics team dedicated to interdisciplinary learning. Our focus spans CAD design, prototype fabrication utilizing CNC and manual tools, and software development with VS Code and C++.
Renewable Energy Club
The Renewable Energy Club at MSOE (MSOE REC) aims to assist students (both within and external to MSOE) in finding and participating in hands-on opportunities to establish/expand their experience in the renewable energy sector. We also seek to educate students about developing technologies in the field. We satisfy these goals through corporate and academic partnerships, networking events, competition, and volunteering in order to encourage interest and pursuit of a career in the renewable energy field.
Society of Automotive Engineers: "Raider Racing" Baja Club Team
Our organization designs, fabricates and races a mini baja style vehicle against other colleges on a state level and a national level.
Society of Automotive Engineers: Formula Hybrid
MSOE SAE Formula Hybrid focuses to design and build a formula-style hybrid race car to compete annually at the SAE-sanctioned international competition. This team serves the purpose of providing an outlet for students to gain hands-on experience for the skills learned in class, to promote teamwork in a multi-cultural and multi-major environment, and to provide team members with added skills and experience to enhance the team member’s employment value.
SuperMileage Team
Supermileage is an engineering design competition open to all students. The team provides participants with the opportunity to enhance their engineering design and project management skills by applying learned classroom theories in a challenging competition. The engineering design goal for Supermileage is to develop and construct a single-person, fuel-efficient vehicle that complies with the Shell Eco-marathon competition rules. The vehicles will run a specified course to obtain the highest combined miles per gallon rating. Students have the opportunity to set a world fuel economy record and increase public awareness of fuel economy and energy-efficient automotive technology.
Steel Bridge
Steel Bridge is a student-run organization, with a school advisor, that spends the whole year designing, fabricating, and constructing a bridge out of steel based on requirements given at the beginning of that academic year. During the month of April, the teams go to the regional competition where they test their bridge and compete against other schools.
Space Raiders (NASA Lunar Robotics)
The MSOE Space Raiders are a competition robotics team that participate in the NASA Lunabotics competition. The objective of the competition is to use NASA project management techniques and apply engineering skills to design, manufacture, and assemble a prototype rover for a simulated lunar environment.
Water and Environmental Team
The MSOE Water and Environmental Team (WET) is a professional student organization dedicated to preparing students for careers in water resources and environmental engineering through educational activities, hands-on experiences, and regional competitions. As a student chapter of the Water Environment Federation (WEF), we are also affiliated with the Central States Water & Environment Association (CSWEA). Our members strive to enhance their understanding of Earth's water systems and complex environment through engineering principles, while also engaging with the community through local volunteerism and outreach.
